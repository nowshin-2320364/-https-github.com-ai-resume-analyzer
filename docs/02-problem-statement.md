# Problem Statement

## The Core Conflict
Modern recruitment relies heavily on Applicant Tracking Systems (ATS) to filter the immense volume of daily applications. However, legacy ATS parsers operate on rigid, rule-based keyword extraction. Highly qualified candidates are frequently filtered out due to minor formatting deviations, use of synonyms (e.g., "GCP" instead of "Google Cloud"), or complex layout structures.

## The Candidate Dilemma
Students and job seekers lack access to enterprise-grade ATS environments to test their resumes before applying. Furthermore, when rejected, they receive zero contextual feedback. They do not know if they were missing a specific technical skill, if their phrasing was too passive, or if their resume format simply broke the parsing engine. This creates a high-friction, low-transparency hiring ecosystem.
